What's the game about?

Soap eliminates 99% of germs.
You are the surviving 1%, the Germ1%.

In this top-down shooter you'll be the last germ standing, blasting at approaching bubbles in order to survive as long as possible.

Hit bubbles with the right color to trigger a chain reaction!
How long can you survive from the bubbles?

MalagaJam Weekend 19

Because I'm in the fourth year of my education, I went to Malaga with my class for a week. It was super fun and Malaga was very beautiful!

The main event during the trip was the MalagaJam, which took place from January 24 to 26, 2025.
There were 450 participants at the MalagaJam and the theme was "Bubbles".

I collaborated with a friend and 4 talented Spanish participants to develop a game with Unity.
I'm very proud of what we've made within 48 hours and I enjoyed the MalagaJam a lot.

What did I learn?

Working together as a complete team

We had 2 programmers, a UI designer, a sprite artist, a cinematic artist and an audio designer.
This meant that we can create a complete game with the roles we have. Designing the game idea went pretty smooth and even the dividing of roles went nicely.

After 48 hours of working on the game, we managed to have the following:
- Trailer for the game
- Game intro + main menu & settings menu
- Translation options for English and Spanish
- Infinite gameplay loop with waves of enemies
- Sprites for every asset
- Music for the game and many sound effects

MalagaJam is amazing

During the MalagaJam they hosted fun events like frisbee, ping pong and a bingo. You could say that they were really good at distracting us from working at the game, but I didn't mind it at all and participated in all of them.

After 48 hours of hard work, the MalagaJam ended and you could walk around freely looking at everyone's games. It was cool to see what everyone made and some games were super impressive that they were made in 48 hours.

I would've never found out about the MalagaJam without the school trip and I would love to go again next year.
